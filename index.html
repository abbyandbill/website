Hello readers!


This blog contains photos of Abby, Bill, and their time together. It was created as a "birthday surprise" for Abby in 2021, and will be updated periodically going forward. We met on Feb 7, 2017 in Ithaca NY while both attending grad school at Cornell, and have been happily together since. Below are links to navigate to photos from a particular year.


Currently, Abby works as a consultant in Washington, DC. In her spare time, she visits alpaca farms, paints, and goes on walks. Bill is writing his PhD dissertation on earthquake physics, and prepping for his next stint as an algorithmic trader in Chicago.

Greg visits again! We spent some time in Ithaca before a night in NYC, and then to California! I must mention our memorable evening with an Airbnb host whose name I shall not mention but rhymes with hell. 2019

2019 got off to a snowy start. Entrance to the apartment was covered in salt particles from the boots, and digging out the cars became part of our routine. This would be Abby's final semester at Cornell. Bill started the year after deciding to give up on academia and started preparing for quantitative finance interviews. 01/21
For Abby's birthday, I used credit card points to buy us plane tickets to Oahu! We both celebrated a break from Ithaca and got on an 8-hour direct flight to Honolulu. A few of the memorable hikes we went on were Kuli'ou'ou Ridge 02/23, Koko Crater 02/24, and Diamond Head (not shown here). During Koko Crater, Abby climbed 80% of the stairs before telling me to go on without her (3rd panel). Eventually she powered through and we were both rewarded with a breathtaking view featuring the island with the ocean as the backdrop. This is what makes Hawaiian hikes stand out from ones in national parks that we've done in the Continental US: the smell of ocean air at the apex. Temperatures would also cool down quite a bit, given the change in altitude. Our intensive hiking routine justified our many massages. Pictured was a particularly stylish massage room with a view. Our Airbnb host graciously gave us a big discount after we fought honorably against the ants in the room. After a few memorable days, we packed our bags and headed back to Ithaca. We both were dying to get back to our snow shoveling routines (not!).



To this day Bill maintains a neutral-positive outlook on his time in college. But for some reason, we would make a yearly pilgrammage back to Swarthmore. On this year's trip, we had exhausted any sight-seeing opporunities from previous visits, so we simply studied in the engineering library (named Cornell btw) and Abby's battle with a grape is captured a cute video below.
The semester was special being Abby's last and marking the start of us going long-distance. But the process was every bit as eventful. We spent a lot of time planning to show Abby's parents around DC and NYC, as well as the final graduation weekend in Ithaca (we had a Google sheet and all). Bill volunteered to cook for Abby's parents, since it's the least he could do to repay them for all the chicken noodle and fancy dining they took him to during his HK visits. He decided to make steak and salmon for Mr and Ms Yip, respectively, and spent the whole semester perfecting his craft, but still managed to overcook the steak in the end (lol).
This is our entourage on the Brooklyn bridge 05/23 and the Newman overlook at Cornell 05/24. Two days later, we all watched with pride and sweat beads as Abby put on her PhD gown and received her well-deserved diploma. I snatched a picture of Mr Yip taking a picture of Abby, with Greg making a funny face on the side. 05/26
After this ~10 day trip, Bill parted ways with the Yips at the Newark airport and started his summer internship at a startup hedge fund while Abby went home with her family for a short break before Greg's graduation and her exciting new job at Mercer in DC. In 2 short weeks, the entourage would be back at it for Greg's graduation from UCSD! What a time to be a Yip parent. Two prestigous degrees collected within 2 weeks woohoo. 06/16

Abby vs. grape @ Swarthmore

Abby began her job as an associate on the Workforce Strategy & Analytics team at Mercer sometime in July. Here she is showing Bill around her office. 07/17
In the fall semester, we'd commute back and forth between Ithaca and DC. The trips were made a lot easier after Bill started to carpool with his labmate, which also made for some interesting discussions on China and Taiwan. For Thanksgiving, we went to Zion with Bill's good friend from college and soon realized this is where the Macbook's default background photo was from. Holy cow. One of our most memorable hikes was to the Narrows. A day before the hike there was a huge snowstorm, which meant the bus that took us to the hike could only drop us off 4 miles away. So we hiked through the snow in our drysuits just to get the Narrows. Walking through the river and not being able to see your steps is quite a tall order. But we managed to live and tell the tale. 11/28
We ended the year in DC. Bill's mom came to visit, so we were treated to some delicious homecooked meals for a couple of weeks. Here is a photo of Abby during our walk around the US Capitol. 12/28


2020

For Abby's birthday, we went to throw axes in Syracuse. The axe itself is about 5 lbs, and the trick to throwing well is to pin down a routine and stay consistent. We eventually both got a bull's eye. For a video of one of Abby's throw see below.
Rising Covid cases halted the US economy, but not the DC cherry blossoms at tidal basin. A year later as I'm typing this blog and we can finally see light at the end of the tunnel, the cherry blossoms are just a month away. 03/14
During the lockdown, one of the few highlights for us was the wedding of Tayler and Tyler. Tayler and Abby had been friends for most of grad school. We had gotten to know them better through sushi dates in Miyaki and Sumo and were excited to be a part of their hybrid wedding, in the backyard of Tyler's parents in Elmira and joined remoted by friends around the world. After the ceremony, we ordered our go-to meal (sushi of course) and took this photo. 06/21
As Bill finished his internship and moved to DC for his final year of grad school, we now call home a cozy little studio in DuPont circle. Greg's annual pilgrimage to Abby back in March got canceled due to Covid, so instead he came for two short visits in the fall. Here are the siblings at tidal basin, on a chill autumn day. 10/01
We eventually discovered the Mt. Vernon trail, a scenic route along the Potomac River extending from Rosslyn past Alexandria. We would rent a bike and ride for a couple of hours before heading back. 11/14

Abby completing her bodyguard training course @ Syracuse



For Thanksgiving this year, we chose to go visit Philly over Virginia Beach with Greg. This is mainly because Greg had never visited Philly, and only in small part because of Bill's urge to revisit his alma mater. We ended up getting takeout from many interesting restaurants while holed up in a nice little apartment just south of Downtown. During the trip Greg was diligently studying for O-chem, which he eventually did very well on. And during one of our walks to the Philly art museum we got a photo on Ben Franklin Pkwy. Bill made an effort to take a photo of the boathouses at night (they're decorated by LED lights and look very pretty), but some road was blocked so he couldn't get the photo. Maybe this is enough reason to revisit? 11/28
The last trip of the year we took was to Petersburg, WV. We did a few hikes, had a scare when our Honda Civic got stuck in snow on a mountain, and made s'mores by the fire with a couple of friends. It was a timely gettaway after a pretty ordinary stay-at-home Christmas. 12/27
